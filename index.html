Context

Who I am as a leader

I lead global technology delivery at scale, with a focus on making complex software change land cleanly in markets. My work sits at the intersection of product, architecture, deployment, and market readiness — ensuring that what we design centrally actually works for restaurants, crew, and guests.

  • Built my career across  product, architecture, and deployment — from mobile app frontend and backend to global deployment.
  • Known for  strategic problem-solving  and  turning ambiguity into clear plans  that teams can rally around.
  • Comfortable navigating  complex, global environments  and balancing standards with local realities.
  • I care deeply about  delivery quality, resilience, and learning — not just "go-live" moments.

Leadership approach

How I lead & what you can expect

Strategy into execution

  • Balance long-term architecture with clear near-term business impact.
  • Use data and scenario thinking to make decisions under pressure.
  • Break complex problems into sharp, achievable milestones.

Safety & learning culture

  • Encourage experimentation with a "fail fast, recover, and learn" mindset.
  • Prefer blameless reviews that focus on systems, not individuals.
  • Surface issues early so we can course-correct while options are still open.

Clear decisions & communication

  • Communicate with a straightforward, transparent style — no drama, no hidden agendas.
  • Translate technical topics into clear trade-offs and stories leaders can act on.
  • Prefer clear ownership, decision rights, and definitions of "done".

Working together

How we work best together

If we're working closely together, here's what you can expect from me — and what I'll expect from you — so we can move fast with trust.

Align early on "what good looks like"

I move fastest when we are clear on  goals, constraints, and success measures . If we're misaligned on those, I will slow us down to fix it.

Bring data and real voices

I value  data, customer impact, and market perspective  over opinions. Challenge my thinking, especially when you see different risks or opportunities.

Ownership and autonomy

I want teams to  own problems end-to-end . I will be clear on direction and decision rights, then give room for you and your teams to solve.

Real-time, two-way feedback

Please give  direct, timely feedback — for example, if I'm moving too fast, missing a stakeholder, or something doesn't land well with your teams. I will do the same in return.

Where partnership matters

Where I need your partnership

I do my best work when we're structured, data-informed, and focused on outcomes. These are the spaces where strong partners around me make the biggest difference.

Slow, ownerless decisions

Long, circular discussions without clear owners, timelines, or criteria make it harder for me to be effective.

How you can help:  Propose an owner, a decision date, and a small set of options with trade-offs.

Debates without data

Opinion-heavy debates without data, guest impact, or real stakeholder voices are less energizing for me.

How you can help:  Pause to gather the right input, then come back with a sharper, evidence-based conversation.

Work that is only political

Navigating politics is part of the job, but work that feels only political and not outcome-focused tends to drain me.

How you can help:  Partner on stakeholder mapping, coalition-building, and narratives that connect to real impact.

In all of these spaces, I work best with  peers and my leadership team  who complement my focus on strategy, delivery, and product/technology depth so we cover the whole field together.
